Kerala FAQs

What is Kerala famous for?

Kerala is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, tourist attractions, historical monuments, beautiful beaches, backwaters, houseboat excursions, heavily wooded mountain ranges, and deciduous semi-deciduous jungles that are home to certain endangered plant and animal species.

What is the best season to visit kerala?

Kerala is best visited between September and March. You will experience a cosy atmosphere and nice weather with less humidity during the winter. Kerala's lagoons, peaceful canals, and spicy plants are best explored in the winter.For ayurvedic treatments, people favour the monsoon season from June to August.

How many minimum days are required to explore Kerala?

The best tourist destinations in Kerala, such as Munnar, Thekkady, and Alleppey, may be explored in 4 days. 8 to 9 days are sufficient for enjoyable family vacations, and 4 to 5 days and 3 nights are sufficient for a romantic getaway for newlyweds. You can prolong this vacation for two or three extra days if you wish to experience well-known adventure sports and unusual locations.

What is the nearest airport for Kerala?

In Kerala, there are three main airports. Trivandrum International Airport, Vallakkadavu (IATA: TRV), Cochin International Airport Limited, Nedumbassery - CIAL, and Calicut International Airport, Karipur (IATA: CCJ) The Cochin International Airport Limited is the busiest and has the most regular airline services to all the main cities in India and the rest of the globe.

What is the local food of Kerala?

Kerala is referred to as India's spice capital. It is also known for its long tradition of delicious food, particularly seafood. Spicy variations of non-vegetarian chicken, meat, and fish (including "Molly" fish and "Karimeen Pollichathu") are well known. Vegetarians can sample coconut and banana-based dishes from Kerala, such as "Puttu-Kadala" and "Appam-Ishtu." Don't forget to enjoy the unique Keralan treats Palada Payasam and Halwa.
